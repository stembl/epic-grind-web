The Methodology
△
Adaptive Intake

Five calibration movements at session one capture real performance data — not a self-reported level. Every target derives from that baseline.

■
3 + 1 Weekly Rhythm

Three strength sessions, one Zone 2 alternative day. Research-backed habit formation — 3–4×/week is the optimal frequency window.

◆
Push · Pull · Lower · Core

Every session trains all four movement patterns. Nothing accumulates imbalance. Muscle groups recover while others work.

↻
Monthly Periodization

Month A builds neural strength (4–8 reps, 3–4 sets). Month B builds structural mass (8–15 reps, 2–3 sets). Alternating captures both adaptations.

△
No Buckets

Foundation, Challenger, and Veteran are labels, not ceilings. The prescription engine places every user on a continuous scale from the first rep.

★
High School → Military

Range validated against NSCA youth standards, AAP guidelines, and Army AFT requirements. One engine, the full spectrum.

Three Tiers — One Continuous Scale
Foundation
High School · Novice

20–30 min sessions. 2–3 sets. 5–9 reps. Total-body compound movements. Form before load. NSCA & AAP backed.

Challenger
Active Adult · Intermediate

35–50 min sessions. 3 sets. 10–15 reps. Push/Pull/Legs split with antagonist supersets. Monthly A/B periodization.

Veteran
Military · Athlete · Advanced

50–60 min sessions. 4 sets. 15–20 reps. AFT-aligned, concurrent training, 4-week mesocycles, deload built in.
